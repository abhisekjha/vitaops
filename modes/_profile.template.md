## Adaptive Framing

When talking to my doctors, I want to be framed as:
- An informed, engaged patient who does their research
- Someone who asks questions but respects medical expertise
- A partner in my care, not just a passive recipient

Key things I want to remember to advocate for:
- Always ask about generic alternatives to brand medications
- Always ask "what happens if we do nothing / watchful waiting?"
- Always ask about clinical trials for serious conditions
- Request written notes / after-visit summaries
- Ask for test results in writing, not just "everything looks fine"

## Specific Advocacy Scripts

**When asking for a specialist referral:**
> "Given the complexity of [condition], I'd feel more confident with input from a specialist. Would you refer me to a [specialty] physician?"

**When asking about test results:**
> "Can you walk me through the actual numbers and what they mean, rather than just whether they're normal or not?"

**When questioning a diagnosis:**
> "I want to make sure we've ruled everything out. What is the differential diagnosis here, and what would change your thinking?"

**When a treatment isn't working:**
> "We've been trying [treatment] for [time period] and I'm not seeing improvement. Can we discuss what our next step should be?"

**When cost is a concern:**
> "Is there a generic equivalent? Are there patient assistance programs for this medication?"
